    US and UK flight ban on electronic devices explained
   
     21 March 2017
    
    Last updated at 18:50 GMT
   
     The United States and United Kingdom have announced that laptops, e-readers and almost any other electronic device that is not a phone will be banned from cabin luggage on some flights.
    
     The US rule applies to 10 airports, the UK to six - including Turkey and middle-eastern countries.
    
     Daniel Sandford explains why phones are allowed when bigger devices aren't - and what might have led to the ban.
